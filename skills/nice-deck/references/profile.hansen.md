# Eric Hansen deck profile

This profile describes how Eric tends to present. It does not prescribe a
theme. The content and the art-direction conversation still determine each
deck's visual world.

## Collaboration

- Bring two or three strong rendered directions, not a questionnaire about
  colors and fonts.
- The central metaphor, mood, and graphic world are collaborative decisions.
- React to feedback as design input. Do not defend an unsuccessful visual.
- Make one representative slide excellent before producing the deck.
- When adapting an existing deck, preserve its titles, structure, and visual
  framing unless Eric explicitly asks for a redesign.

## Slide voice

- Graphical and minimal. Slides support a speaker; they are not documents to
  read aloud.
- Dry and declarative. Short sentences, concrete nouns, no marketing gloss.
- Lead with the payoff when the material supports it.
- Use the visual to explain or create emotion. Do not add copy to compensate for
  a weak composition.
- Delete filler copy and slides that do not advance the argument.
- Keep exact technical strings, names, URLs, and commands native and sourced.

## Visual preferences

- Clean hierarchy, strong contrast, and readable projection-scale type.
- Original graphics are welcome, including AI-generated work, when they carry
  real meaning.
- Light or dark is a consequence of the viewing scene, not a default.
- Minimal does not mean faint, empty, generic, or under-designed.
- A deck may vary composition slide to slide while retaining one coherent voice.
- Reusing a color asserts shared meaning. Use one color for peers, distinct
  colors for distinct categories, and never cycle colors decoratively.
- Let headings use the available width. Do not force a title or subtitle to wrap
  with an arbitrary narrow max-width.
- Avoid repeated gray containers, dividers, and rules. A boundary must explain
  grouping or hierarchy.

## Evidence and links

- Verify claims against authoritative sources. Keep a slide about a product
  scoped to what that product actually does.
- Capture the canonical landing or catalog page, not a convenient subpage.
  Wait for the page to finish loading and use the intended theme.
- On resource and marketplace slides, keep the linked title, a useful
  description, and the written canonical URL.
- Omit a trailing slash from authored and displayed URLs unless the destination
  requires it.

## Delivery

- Before placing artifacts in OneDrive, inspect the existing presentation root
  and follow its folder and filename conventions.
- Eric's presentation folders are date-first: `YYYY-MM-DD Presentation Name`.
- Keep the HTML, its local assets, and the email-safe PDF together. Read the
  packaged HTML back from that folder and remove duplicate copies created during
  the session.
- The HTML is the source of truth. A PDF should capture the rendered slides
  exactly and preserve clickable links rather than reflow the deck.

## Avoid

- Fixed design-language or template selection.
- Topic-literal reflexes such as space for AI or terminal-dark for developer
  tools.
- SaaS cards, glowing networks, dot-and-line filler, decorative glass, warm
  paper defaults, and generic corporate stock.
- Walls of text, copied draft prose, undersized metadata, or low-contrast gray.
- Decorative dividers, repeated gray boxes, and arbitrary category colors.
- Showing a slide that has not been freshly rendered, inspected, and refreshed
  in Canvas.
